CAMBODIA

The best time to visit Cambodia is during the dry season from November to April. The weather is pleasant with less humidity, making it ideal for exploring temples and outdoor activities. December to February offers the coolest temperatures.

Most visitors need a visa to enter Cambodia. You can obtain a tourist visa (T-class) on arrival at major entry points or apply for an e-Visa online before your trip. The tourist visa is valid for 30 days and can be extended once for an additional 30 days.

Cambodia uses the Cambodian Riel (KHR), but US Dollars are widely accepted throughout the country. Most prices in tourist areas are quoted in dollars. ATMs dispense both currencies. It is recommended to carry small denominations of both.

Cambodia is generally safe for tourists. Like any destination, exercise common sense precautions: watch your belongings in crowded areas, avoid walking alone late at night in unfamiliar areas, and use reputable transportation services. The Cambodian people are known for their warm hospitality.

Top attractions include Angkor Wat temple complex in Siem Reap, the Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda in Phnom Penh, the beaches of Sihanoukville and Koh Rong, the riverside town of Kampot, Bokor Mountain, and the floating villages of Tonle Sap Lake.

The official language is Khmer (Cambodian). English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. French is still spoken by some older generations due to colonial history. Learning a few basic Khmer phrases is appreciated by locals.

When visiting temples, dress modestly. Cover your shoulders and knees - no tank tops, short skirts, or shorts above the knee. Remove shoes and hats before entering sacred areas. Some temples provide sarongs or coverings at the entrance if needed.

Cambodian cuisine features fresh ingredients with rice as a staple. Popular dishes include Fish Amok (fish curry in banana leaves), Lok Lak (stir-fried beef), Kuy Teav (noodle soup), and fresh Kampot pepper crab. Street food is delicious and affordable throughout the country.

Transportation options include tuk-tuks (motor rickshaws) for short distances, buses for intercity travel, domestic flights between major cities, and boats on Tonle Sap. Ride-hailing apps like Grab and PassApp are popular in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap.

Recommended vaccinations include Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and routine vaccinations. Consult your doctor about Japanese Encephalitis and Rabies vaccines if traveling to rural areas. Malaria prophylaxis may be advised for certain regions. Always carry mosquito repellent.

Iconic Places to Visit

From royal palaces to hidden gems, explore the best of Phnom Penh

What is the most iconic landmark in Phnom Penh?
+

The Royal Palace is undoubtedly Phnom Penh's crown jewel. Built in 1866, this stunning complex features the Silver Pagoda with its floor of 5,000 silver tiles, the Throne Hall, and beautifully manicured gardens. Visit early morning for the best photos and fewer crowds.

Where can I learn about Cambodia's history?
+

The Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum and Choeung Ek Killing Fields provide sobering insights into Cambodia's tragic past under the Khmer Rouge. The National Museum showcases 14,000+ artifacts spanning centuries of Khmer civilization and artistry.

What is Wat Phnom and why is it significant?
+

Wat Phnom is the legendary temple that gave Phnom Penh its name. Sitting atop a 27-meter hill, it marks the spot where Lady Penh found Buddha statues floating in the river. The temple grounds are perfect for peaceful morning walks and sunset views.

Where should I go for nightlife and entertainment?
+

Bassac Lane offers trendy cocktail bars and live music. Street 308 is the backpacker hub with lively bars. For upscale experiences, try the Eclipse Sky Bar or rooftop lounges along the riverfront with stunning Mekong views.

What markets should I visit for authentic experiences?
+

Central Market (Phsar Thmei) is an Art Deco masterpiece perfect for jewelry and souvenirs. Russian Market (Tuol Tom Poung) offers vintage items and local crafts. For food adventures, explore the bustling Orussey Market at dawn.

Is the Riverfront worth visiting?
+

Absolutely! Sisowath Quay runs along the Tonle Sap River and is the heart of Phnom Penh's social life. Enjoy sunset walks, riverside dining, street food vendors, and watch locals exercise in the evening breeze.

Where can I see traditional Cambodian performances?
+

The Cambodian Living Arts center hosts authentic Apsara dance shows. Phare Circus at the Factory combines circus arts with Cambodian storytelling. Sovanna Phum Arts Association performs traditional shadow puppet shows.

What are some hidden gems tourists often miss?
+

Explore the abandoned French colonial buildings on Street 106, visit the serene Wat Langka temple, or discover the art galleries along Street 240. The Koh Pich (Diamond Island) development offers a glimpse of modern Cambodia.

Where can I enjoy nature within the city?
+

Hun Sen Park offers green spaces and jogging paths. The Royal Palace gardens are beautifully maintained. For wildlife, visit Phnom Tamao Wildlife Rescue Center about 40km outside the city to see rescued elephants and sun bears.

What religious sites should I explore beyond Wat Phnom?
+

Wat Ounalom is the headquarters of Cambodian Buddhism. Wat Botum features beautiful murals. The stunning Wat Moha Montrei showcases traditional Khmer architecture, and Preah Sisowath Quay Pagoda offers riverside serenity.

Where can I take the best photos of the city?
+

The Koh Pich observation deck offers panoramic views. Sunrise at the riverfront creates golden reflections. The Independence Monument is stunning at sunset. For street photography, the chaotic energy of Kandal Market is unbeatable.

Are there any island escapes near Phnom Penh?
+

Koh Dach (Silk Island) is just a short ferry ride away, famous for silk weaving villages and peaceful bike rides. Koh Pich (Diamond Island) is an urban island development with parks, restaurants, and entertainment venues.

What architectural styles can I see in Phnom Penh?
+

Phnom Penh showcases remarkable diversity: French colonial villas on Norodom Boulevard, Art Deco Central Market, New Khmer Architecture from the 1960s like the Olympic Stadium, and the classical Khmer style of the Royal Palace.

Where should I go for sunset views?
+

The FCC rooftop terrace overlooking the river is legendary. Le Moon at Amanjaya Hotel offers romantic riverside dining. For locals' experience, join the crowds at Chroy Changvar peninsula where two rivers meet.

What day trips can I take from Phnom Penh?
+

Visit the Oudong ancient royal capital (40km), explore the pre-Angkorian Tonle Bati temples, or take a boat to the floating villages of Kampong Chhnang. The Mekong island of Koh Okhna Tey offers rustic charm.

Where can I experience local neighborhood life?
+

Wander through BKK1 (Boeung Keng Kang) for cafe culture and boutiques. Explore Tuol Tompong for authentic street food. The Chbar Ampov area across the river shows traditional Khmer village life within the city limits.

What museums are worth visiting besides the main ones?
+

The Train Museum at the old railway station is a hidden gem. The Cambodia-Vietnam Friendship Monument tells diplomatic history. Bophana Audiovisual Resource Center preserves Cambodia's film and music heritage.

Culinary Adventures

From street food stalls to fine dining, taste the flavors of Cambodia

What is the must-try Cambodian dish in Phnom Penh?
+

Fish Amok is Cambodia's national treasure - a creamy coconut curry steamed in banana leaves. Try it at Romdeng or Malis restaurant for refined versions, or at local shops near Central Market for authentic street-style preparations.

Where can I find the best street food?
+

Street 136 near the riverfront is street food paradise. Kandal Market serves incredible Kuy Teav (noodle soup) at dawn. The night market on Street 106 offers grilled meats, fresh seafood, and local desserts until late.

What are the best fine dining restaurants?
+

Malis by celebrity chef Luu Meng offers elevated Khmer cuisine. Topaz serves French fine dining. Embassy by The Quay features innovative fusion. Yi Sang at Rosewood offers stunning Chinese cuisine with river views.

Where should I go for breakfast?
+

Brown Coffee is Cambodia's beloved chain with excellent pastries. ARTillery Cafe serves creative brunch in an art space. For local style, try Bai Sach Chrouk (pork and rice) from any street vendor - a beloved morning ritual.

What restaurants support social causes?
+

Romdeng employs former street youth and serves traditional cuisine. Friends (Mith Samlanh) trains marginalized young people. Daughters of Cambodia cafe supports trafficking survivors. Hagar Catering College graduates at-risk women.

Where can I find vegetarian and vegan options?
+

Vibe Cafe is 100% vegan with creative dishes. Karma offers vegetarian Indian and Western fare. ARTillery has great plant-based options. Buddhist temple restaurants near Wat Botum serve meat-free meals.

What is Lok Lak and where should I try it?
+

Lok Lak is a beloved stir-fried beef dish with pepper-lime dipping sauce, often served with a fried egg. Try it at Friends restaurant, Sovanna II in BKK1, or at any local restaurant - it's a Cambodian comfort food staple.

Where can I try Khmer BBQ?
+

Srey Mom BBQ on Street 310 is a local favorite. Sovanna BBQ near Russian Market is popular with Cambodians. These places offer tableside grills where you cook meat, seafood, and vegetables in a fun social setting.

What desserts should I try?
+

Num Ansom (sticky rice cakes) are found at markets. Fried bananas with ice cream at any street stall. Tuk-a-loc (Cambodian fruit shakes) from vendors everywhere. Beng Mealea Cafe serves excellent traditional sweets.

Where can I find good coffee?
+

Brown Coffee is Cambodia's Starbucks rival. Gloria Jean's and Amazon are popular chains. For specialty coffee, try Backyard Cafe or Lot 369. Cambodian iced coffee with condensed milk is a sweet, strong local favorite.

What international cuisines are available?
+

Phnom Penh is incredibly diverse. Japanese at Cho Mee, Korean at Kimbap Town, Thai at Smile Restaurant, Indian at Sher-e-Punjab, Italian at Pop Cafe, Mexican at Tacos Kokopelli. BKK1 area has the widest selection.

Where should I go for seafood?
+

Kep crab restaurants on Street 21 serve the famous Kampot pepper crab. Boat Noodle on the riverfront has fresh fish. Night market grilled seafood stalls offer incredibly fresh catches at local prices.

What is Kuy Teav and where is the best?
+

Kuy Teav is Cambodia's breakfast noodle soup - clear broth with pork, herbs, and rice noodles. The most famous spot is Heng Kuy Teav near Olympic Stadium. Central Market vendors and Kandal Market shops are also excellent.

Where can I take a cooking class?
+

Frizz Restaurant offers morning classes with market visits. Cambodia Cooking Class near Russian Market is highly rated. Romdeng runs occasional workshops. These include market tours to learn about local ingredients.

What should I drink in Cambodia?
+

Angkor Beer is the national brew. Fresh coconut water from street vendors is refreshing. Sugar cane juice is everywhere. For cocktails, try infusions with Kampot pepper or palm wine at craft cocktail bars in BKK1.

Where should I go for riverside dining?
+

FCC Restaurant has colonial charm with river views. Le Moon offers romantic sunset dining. Boat restaurants on the Mekong serve seafood with floating ambiance. Sisowath Quay has dozens of riverside terraces.

What food should I avoid or be careful with?
+

Avoid ice from unknown sources and raw vegetables at very basic stalls. Prahok (fermented fish paste) is an acquired taste. Deep-fried insects are safe but adventurous. Stick to busy stalls with high turnover for freshness.

Getting Around

Navigate the city like a local with these transportation tips

How do I get from the airport to the city?
+

Phnom Penh International Airport is 10km from the center. Official airport taxis cost $12-15 fixed rate. Grab/PassApp rides are $7-10. Some hotels offer free pickup. The journey takes 30-45 minutes depending on traffic.

What is the best way to get around the city?
+

Grab and PassApp ride-hailing apps are the most convenient and affordable options. Tuk-tuks are fun for short trips ($2-4 around town). Motorbike taxis (moto-dops) are cheapest but require confidence. Walking is great for riverside areas.

Can I rent a motorbike or bicycle?
+

Motorbikes rent for $5-8/day from shops near popular areas. Helmets are mandatory. Traffic is chaotic so only rent if experienced. Bicycles from hotels or rental shops cost $2-5/day and are great for riverside exploration.

How do I travel to other cities from Phnom Penh?
+

Giant Ibis and Mekong Express run comfortable buses to Siem Reap (6 hrs), Sihanoukville (4 hrs), and Kampot (3 hrs). Book online or at travel agencies. Shared taxis and minivans are faster but less comfortable.

Is there a city bus system?
+

Yes! Phnom Penh City Bus launched in 2014 with routes covering major areas. Buses are air-conditioned with fares of 1,500 Riel ($0.37). Routes are limited but expanding. Google Maps shows some routes, or ask locals for help.

How much should I pay for a tuk-tuk?
+

Short trips within districts: $2-3. Cross-city journeys: $4-6. Full day hire: $25-35. Always negotiate before riding. Using apps like Grab shows fair prices. Riverside drivers often charge more - walk a block inland for better rates.

What is the most enjoyable way to explore the city?
+

Cyclos (bicycle rickshaws) offer a slow, romantic way to see the city - perfect for the riverfront. Walking tours through BKK1 and the colonial quarter reveal hidden details. Sunset boat rides on the Mekong are magical.

Unforgettable Experiences

Create lasting memories with these activities and adventures

What unique experiences should I not miss?
+

Watch sunrise at the Royal Palace, take a sunset Mekong cruise, attend an Apsara dance performance, join morning aerobics with locals at the riverfront, or explore night markets for street food adventures.

What cultural activities can I participate in?
+

Take a Khmer cooking class at Frizz restaurant. Learn traditional dance at Cambodian Living Arts. Join meditation sessions at Wat Langka. Visit silk weaving villages on Koh Dach island for hands-on textile experiences.

Where can I experience Phnom Penh's art scene?
+

Sa Sa Bassac gallery showcases contemporary Cambodian art. Meta House screens films and hosts exhibitions. Phare Circus combines performance art with social enterprise. Street art tours reveal murals hidden throughout the city.

What outdoor activities are available?
+

Cycle along the Mekong riverside path. Take a kayak tour to nearby islands. Join morning Tai Chi sessions in public parks. The Hash House Harriers organize social running events. Kirirom National Park offers hiking just 2 hours away.

What activities are best for families?
+

Kids love the Dream Land amusement park on Koh Pich. The Cambodia Crocodile Park entertains all ages. Phnom Tamao Wildlife Rescue Center teaches conservation. River cruises and cyclo rides create fun family memories.

How can I give back during my visit?
+

Eat at social enterprise restaurants like Romdeng and Friends. Shop at fair trade stores like Rajana and Daughters of Cambodia. Visit ethical attractions like Phnom Tamao. Many organizations welcome skilled volunteers for longer stays.

What wellness experiences are available?
+

Bodia Spa offers traditional Cambodian treatments using local ingredients. Yoga studios like Yoga Phnom Penh have daily classes. Nail spas and massage parlors are everywhere at great prices. Wat Langka hosts meditation for visitors.

Where can I shop for unique souvenirs?
+

Artisans Angkor sells beautifully crafted replicas. Russian Market has vintage finds and silk. Rajana offers fair-trade crafts. Night markets sell local art and jewelry. Street 240 boutiques feature contemporary Cambodian designers.

What nightlife experiences are memorable?
+

Catch live music at Equinox or Hops Brewery. Dance at clubs along Street 308. Enjoy rooftop cocktails at Eclipse Sky Bar. Take a dinner cruise with traditional performances. Night markets offer food, music, and local atmosphere until late.

What is the best beach for relaxing?

Otres Beach is widely considered the most laid-back and relaxing stretch of sand.

How do I get to Koh Rong?

Take a 45-minute speed ferry from the Serendipity Autonomous Pier.

Is Kbal Chhay Waterfall worth visiting?

Yes, especially during the rainy season when the water flow is powerful and beautiful.

Where is the best panoramic view?

Wat Leu Temple, situated on a hill, offers stunning views of the city and the ocean.

What is the quietest island?

Koh Rong Sanloem is generally quieter and more serene than its larger neighbor.

Where can I experience local daily life?

Phsar Leu Market is the bustling, authentic heart of the city's commerce.

Is Ream National Park open to tourists?

Yes, you can hire boats to explore the mangroves and spot local wildlife.

What's special about Independence Beach?

It holds historic significance from the 1960s and maintains a more secluded, upscale vibe.

Can I visit the Golden Lions Roundabout?

Absolutely, it is the most iconic landmark connecting the city to the main beaches.

Where is the best fresh seafood?

The beach shacks along the coast and Phsar Leu market offer incredible, fresh catches daily.

Are there vegetarian options?

Yes, many cafes and international restaurants cater well to vegetarian diets.

What is a must-try local dish?

You must try the freshly grilled squid with Kampot pepper dipping sauce.

Can I eat right on the beach?

Yes, many spots let you dine with your toes in the sand, particularly around sunset.

Are there high-end dining options?

Yes, the luxury resort areas and major hotels feature premium international dining.

What does a typical meal cost?

Street food runs $2-$5, while sit-down beachfront meals usually range from $8 to $20+.

How do I get around the city?

PassApp and Grab rickshaws are the easiest and most affordable way to navigate the city.

How long is the ferry to the islands?

The speed ferries to Koh Rong and Koh Rong Sanloem take approximately 45 minutes.

Best way to travel from Phnom Penh?

The Expressway is by far the best route, cutting travel time down to just about 2 hours.

Where can I see bioluminescent plankton?

Night boat tours from Koh Rong will take you to dark waters where the plankton glow brightly.

Is scuba diving available?

Yes, there are great dive sites around Koh Tang and Koh Prins for certified divers.

Can I rent jet skis?

Yes, jet ski rentals are readily available at most of the major public beaches.

What is island hopping?

It's a full-day boat tour that takes you to multiple smaller islands for snorkeling and BBQ.

Are there fishing tours?

Yes, night fishing for squid with local fishermen is a highly rated activity.

Where can I get a massage?

Spas are everywhere, ranging from simple beachside massages to luxury hotel treatments.

Is the nightlife active?

Yes, it features a mix of beachside chill bars and larger modern entertainment complexes.

Can I do a mangrove boat tour?

Absolutely, hiring a local boat in Ream National Park to navigate the mangroves is highly recommended.

Are there sunset cruises?

Many operators offer late afternoon cruises equipped with drinks to watch the sun drop into the gulf.

Ancient Sites (17)

What is the best time to see the Angkor Wat sunrise?

Arrive at the ticket checkpoint by 4:45 AM. The best spots by the lily ponds fill up quickly before the sun appears around 5:30 AM.

How many days do I need for the temples?

A 3-day pass is highly recommended to see the major sites without rushing.

Is there a dress code for the temples?

Yes. Shoulders and knees must be covered. A polite dress code is strictly enforced.

Which temple is famous for tree roots?

Ta Prohm is the famous 'Tomb Raider' temple overtaken by massive jungle roots.

What temple has massive faces carved?

The Bayon temple within Angkor Thom features over 200 serene, massive stone faces.

Restaurants (9)

What is 'Fish Amok'?

Cambodia's signature dish: a delicate, curried fish mousse steamed in a banana leaf.

Where is Pub Street?

It is the tourist heart of Siem Reap, famous for cheap beer, loud music, and street food.

Are there good vegetarian options in Khmer cuisine?

Yes! Many curry and vegetable dishes are naturally vegan or can be easily adapted.

Transportation (6)

How much does a Tuk-Tuk cost per day?

A private driver for the main temple circuit typically costs between $15 and $25 USD per day.

Do I need a local SIM card to use ride-hailing apps?

Yes, having a local number is essential for apps like PassApp or Grab.

Is it easy to rent bicycles?

Very easy. Many guesthouses offer them for $2-5 per day. Electric bikes are also available.

Activities (10)

What is the Phare Cambodian Circus?

An incredible modern circus combining theater, music, and acrobatics to tell Cambodian stories. It's a non-profit supporting young artists.

Where can I see an Apsara dance show?

Many hotels and restaurants offer traditional Khmer dinner shows with Apsara performances.

Can I visit the floating villages on Tonle Sap Lake?

Yes, Kampong Phluk and Chong Kneas are popular boat trips to see unique stilted communities.
